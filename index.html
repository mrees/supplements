Supplements Wiki

Evidence-based supplement guidance — with a focus on adults aged 65 and older

This wiki summarises current scientific evidence for commonly used dietary supplements. Each page covers benefits, recommended dosages by age group, safety considerations, drug interactions, and synergistic combinations. All guidance should be discussed with a physician before use.

How to use this wiki Click any supplement card to read its full evidence review. Pages are cross-linked wherever supplements interact or combine synergistically. Use the sidebar to browse by supplement or topic.

🦴 Bone & Joint Health

Vitamin D
Essential for calcium absorption and bone mineralisation. Deficiency is very common in older adults.
Bone Immune Mood
Calcium
The primary mineral of bone tissue. Supplementation is important when dietary intake is insufficient.
Bone Muscle
Magnesium
Works alongside calcium and vitamin D for bone health. Also supports muscle, nerve, and sleep.
Bone Sleep Heart

💪 Muscle & Physical Performance

Creatine
Strong evidence for preserving muscle mass and strength in older adults. Underused in seniors.
Muscle Brain Bone
Beta-Alanine
Raises muscle carnosine; improves endurance during moderate-to-high intensity exercise.
Endurance Muscle
Nitrates
Dietary nitrates (beetroot) improve blood flow and exercise efficiency, especially in older adults.
Exercise Heart BP

❤️ Heart & Metabolic Health

Omega-3
Marine EPA/DHA reduce triglycerides, support heart rhythm, and reduce inflammatory markers.
Heart Brain Joints
Magnesium
Supports blood pressure regulation and reduces arrhythmia risk.
Heart BP

🧠 Cognition & Nervous System

Vitamin B12
Nerve function and cognition depend on B12. Absorption declines markedly with age.
Cognition Nerves Energy
Folate (B9)
Works with B12 to control homocysteine. Critical for brain health across all life stages.
Cognition Heart DNA
Omega-3
DHA is a structural component of brain cell membranes.
Brain Inflammation

🌿 Digestion & Micronutrients

Psyllium
Soluble fibre with strong evidence for cholesterol reduction and bowel regularity.
Gut Cholesterol Blood Sugar
Zinc
Immune function, wound healing, and taste/smell. Deficiency is common in older adults.
Immune Wound Healing
Iron
Required for red blood cell production. Supplementation only recommended when deficiency is confirmed.
Anaemia Energy
