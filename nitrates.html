Dietary Nitrates

Beetroot and leafy greens improve blood flow, exercise efficiency, and blood pressure — with stronger effects in older adults

Exercise Performance Blood Pressure Heart Health Blood Flow Oxygen Efficiency
Evidence strength:
Strong (exercise O₂ efficiency, BP); effects larger in older adults

Overview

Dietary nitrates are compounds found naturally in high concentrations in beetroot, spinach, rocket (arugula), celery, and other leafy vegetables. In the body, nitrate is converted — via nitrite — to nitric oxide (NO), a signalling molecule that causes blood vessels to dilate. This vasodilation improves blood flow, reduces blood pressure, and lowers the oxygen cost of physical effort.

Nitrate supplements are most commonly taken as concentrated beetroot juice shots (e.g., 70 ml "shots" containing ~400 mg nitrate), beetroot powder, or as direct dietary intake from vegetables. The conversion of nitrate to nitrite requires oral bacteria; using antibacterial mouthwash blocks this pathway and reduces efficacy.

Key fact for seniors The ability to produce nitric oxide through other pathways declines with age, making the dietary nitrate→nitrite→NO pathway increasingly important in older adults. Several studies have found that the exercise performance and blood pressure benefits of nitrate supplementation are greater in older individuals than in younger ones.

What the Evidence Shows

Exercise efficiency and oxygen consumption

One of the most well-replicated findings in sports nutrition is that dietary nitrate reduces the oxygen cost (VO₂) of submaximal exercise by 3–5%. This means the same physical effort requires less oxygen — effectively improving endurance and delaying fatigue. For older adults, this translates to being able to sustain activities (walking, stair climbing, gardening) for longer with less breathlessness.

Blood pressure reduction

Multiple meta-analyses confirm that dietary nitrate (predominantly from beetroot juice) reduces systolic blood pressure by approximately 4–5 mmHg and diastolic by 2–3 mmHg. These reductions are clinically meaningful — comparable to some antihypertensive medications — and occur within 2–3 hours of ingestion.

Functional capacity in older adults

Several trials specifically in older adults show that beetroot juice supplementation improves 6-minute walk distance, sit-to-stand performance, and gait speed — markers closely linked to independence and quality of life. The improvement in muscle efficiency may be partly responsible, alongside better blood flow to exercising muscles.

Cognitive function

Nitric oxide increases cerebral blood flow. Preliminary trials show that acute beetroot juice intake improves cognitive processing speed in older adults. Longer-term studies are needed, but the mechanism is biologically plausible.

Safety & Cautions

Important cautions Beetroot juice causes harmless red or pink colouration of urine and stools (beeturia) — this is not dangerous but can be alarming. Do not use antibacterial mouthwash if taking nitrate supplements for performance — it kills the oral bacteria needed for nitrate-to-nitrite conversion, blocking the effect entirely.
Drug interactions Dietary nitrates interact significantly with PDE5 inhibitors (sildenafil/Viagra, tadalafil/Cialis) — combined use causes a dangerous drop in blood pressure and is contraindicated. Also use with caution alongside antihypertensive medications; the combined blood-pressure-lowering effect may be excessive. Discuss with your physician if you are on any blood pressure treatment.

Synergistic Combinations

  • Nitrates + Creatine: Nitrates improve oxygen delivery and blood flow, while creatine provides rapid energy for muscle contraction. Together, they address different limiting factors in exercise performance and may have additive benefits on overall exercise capacity in older adults.
  • Nitrates + Beta-Alanine: Nitrates reduce the oxygen cost of effort; beta-alanine buffers muscle acidity. They work independently and can be combined for a broader improvement in exercise endurance and tolerance.
  • Nitrates + Omega-3: Both support cardiovascular health through different mechanisms — nitrates via vasodilation and blood pressure, omega-3 via triglyceride reduction and anti-inflammation. Their combined use is likely additive for heart health.
  • Nitrates + Magnesium: Magnesium helps maintain vascular tone and blood pressure; nitrates cause acute vasodilation. Together they provide complementary blood pressure support.
